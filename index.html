  • What you can do

    This will guide in the website to show you what is possible to do. Interest in photography has exploded over the last 10 years, largely thanks to the developments in digital photography. Cameras and computers have become cheaper and more powerful, software more sophistocated and printers can now print photos that are as good (if not better) than anything produced in a chemical darkroom. Now, once you’ve acquired a digital setup, the economic restrictions of film and development costs have been removed and the cost of photography is virtually nul. Along with these developments in photography has been the parallel development of the Web. Ten years ago websites were largely clumsy, HTML driven constructions. Today, contemporary photographers have powerful tools such as Javascript with which they develop their websites. Photographers can also sell their work through companies, and through photo libraries, opening up new revenue streams. The result is that photographers are finding new and exciting ways to showcase their best work online. I took a look at one of the easiest photography applciation that I could find.

    This website works in two steps, the first one is optionnal and the second one is the main step :

    1. If you want some pictures from around you, you can first use the geolocation API from google Maps "Locate me" to find yourself. (this function is more useful if your are using your mobile phone and you don't really know where you are)

    If you are using a web browser don't forget to allow your browser to send your Geolocation to the website

    This step is optionnal, you can do it or not and now go to the second step.

    2. The second step is the main step. This step is using the Instagram and Google Search APIs. If you've done the first step or not you can click on "Change all Pix here !" button. This will show you a pop-up where you can enter a Tag (only one and no spaces allowed). The APIs will automatically find photos from the Instagram and Google databases which are relative to your Tag !

    If you located yourself before and want some pictures relative to what is around you, just take a look at the Map and choose the Tag you want in the " Pix' " tab.

Getting Started

This website allow you to find Pix (photos) on the web. To do this, two web APIs are used : Instagram and Google API. You can also locate yourself with the Google Maps Api if you don't know where you are. This function can be used with your mobile. Go to "How to" tab to learn more.
